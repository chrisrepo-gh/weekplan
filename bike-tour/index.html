Altmühltal Bike Tour

Erlangen → Treuchtlingen → Eichstätt → Kelheim → Saal (Donau) → Ingolstadt → Erlangen · 2 days · normal fitness, first bike tour

Plan at a glance: train out to Treuchtlingen, cycle the flat, dedicated Altmühltal-Radweg to Eichstätt for an Airbnb overnight, then a long second day pushing all the way through the valley to Kelheim and on to Saal (Donau), where a regional train takes the bike straight to Ingolstadt for the connection home. Point-to-point the whole way — no backtracking, no boat.

Day 1 — Wednesday

TimeLeg
08:00Erlangen → Nürnberg Hbf (S1, ~12 min)
~08:30–09:00Change at Nürnberg Hbf → Regional-Express to Treuchtlingen (~55–70 min)
~09:30–10:00Arrive Treuchtlingen, unload bike, start riding
~10:00 onwardCycle Treuchtlingen → Pappenheim → Solnhofen → Dollnstein → Eichstätt ~35 km

Flat, car-free, well-signed dedicated path the whole way. Worth a stop in Solnhofen (fossil/Jura-Museum) en route. Arrive early-mid afternoon with time to explore Eichstätt's baroque old town and Willibaldsburg castle.

Overnight: Eichstätt (Airbnb)
Booked directly by Fernando — cheaper than the DJH hostel once accommodation prices were compared. Staying in Eichstätt itself (rather than pushing on to Kipfenberg) means day 2 is a long one — see the note below.
Heads up — day 2 is a big day: Eichstätt → Kelheim is about 96 km on its own; add the extra ~5.5 km to Saal (Donau) and day 2 is roughly ~101 km in total, versus day 1's ~35 km. It's flat, dedicated path the entire way, but that's a long first "big day" for a first bike tour. Worth starting promptly, keeping stops efficient, and having a mental fallback (e.g. hopping off at Kelheim and taking a taxi/bus to a station if it's not going well) rather than forcing the full distance.

Day 2 — Thursday

TimeLeg
~08:00–09:00Depart Eichstätt as early as comfortable
Cycle Eichstätt → Kipfenberg → Beilngries → Dietfurt → Riedenburg → Essing → Kelheim ~96 km
Continue along the Danube path Kelheim → Saal (Donau) ~5.5 km
hourly, 14:08–21:14Board RB17/RE18 (agilis) at Saal (Donau) → Ingolstadt Hbf bike needs its own ticket, no reservation
Change at Ingolstadt Hbf → Regional-Express to Nürnberg Hbf (~40–45 min, frequent)
Nürnberg Hbf → Erlangen (S1, ~12 min)
Train + bike notes: agilis (operator of RB17/RE18) accepts the Deutschlandticket for the passenger fare, but it does not cover the bike. Each bike needs its own ticket — a "Fahrrad-Tageskarte Bayern" or "Fahrradkarte Nahverkehr," both about €7.50. There's no reservation system and no guaranteed space: bikes are first-come and get bumped in favour of wheelchairs/strollers if the train is full, so a summer Thursday afternoon carries some risk of a full train — worth having a backup (next hourly departure, or the direct-cycle fallback to Regensburg described below) in mind.

Saal (Donau) departures toward Ingolstadt run roughly hourly through the evening: 14:08, 15:14, 16:08, 17:14, 18:08, 19:14, 20:08, 21:14 (weekdays) — plenty of buffer even if day 2 runs long. Ingolstadt → Nürnberg regional trains are frequent (roughly every 30–60 min).

Alternative: the boat route (kept for reference)

An earlier version of this plan had day 2 ending with the Kelheim → Weltenburg boat through the Donaudurchbruch gorge, then a cycle on to Regensburg for the train home. That's a nicer scenic finish (and a good excuse to visit Kloster Weltenburg's monastery brewery) but adds boat-schedule and water-level dependency. The Saal (Donau) route above is simpler and fully train-based — worth keeping the boat option in mind only if day 2 is going well and there's still energy/time for the detour.

  • Kelheim–Weltenburg boat schedule (Donaudurchbruch) — daily in season, bikes €5.50, water-level dependent
  • Google Maps: direct Kelheim → Regensburg cycling fallback (~38 km)

Tickets & logistics

  • Bike + train (Bayern regional lines): ~€1/bike on DB regional trains with a Bayern-weit ticket (e.g. Bayern-Ticket). On agilis (RB17/RE18) it's a separate ~€7.50 bike ticket instead — see above.
  • Rush-hour rule: no bikes on S-Bahn/regional trains Mon–Fri 6:00–8:00. An 8:00 departure clears this.
  • Book the Airbnb — confirm it if not already, July is high season in the Altmühltal.
