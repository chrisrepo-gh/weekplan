Altmühltal Bike Tour

Erlangen → Treuchtlingen → Eichstätt → camp (Inching / Gungolding / Kipfenberg) → Kelheim → Saal (Donau) → Ingolstadt → Erlangen · 2 days · normal fitness, first bike tour

Plan at a glance: train out to Treuchtlingen, cycle the flat, dedicated Altmühltal-Radweg through Eichstätt and on to a tent camping spot chosen on the day depending on energy, then day 2 finishes the valley to Kelheim and continues to Saal (Donau), where a regional train takes the bike straight to Ingolstadt for the connection home. Point-to-point the whole way — no backtracking, no boat.

Flat, car-free, well-signed dedicated path the whole way. Worth a stop in Solnhofen (fossil/Jura-Museum) en route.

Overnight: tent camping — decide on the day
Bringing a small tent instead of booking ahead, so the stopping point flexes with energy on the day. All three sit right on the Altmühltal-Radweg past Eichstätt:
OptionDistance past EichstättFacilitiesPrice
Bootsrastplatz Inching~10.5 km shortest day 1Basic: toilet (no drinking water), fire pit, parking. No showers.€7/tent + €2/person
Bootsrastplatz Gungolding~21.5 km middle optionBasic: toilet, water tap, fire pit, shelter hut. No showers.€7/tent + €2/person
AZUR Camping Altmühltal, Kipfenberg~31 km longest day 1, most comfortFull campground: hot showers, shop, 190 pitches. Max distance for day 1, but properly equipped.Check azur-camping.de for current rates
Both Bootsrastplätze are simple canoeist rest stops (max 2-night stay, open May–Oct) but explicitly also welcome tent-camping individual travellers, not just boats. Worth carrying enough water if stopping at Inching, since it has no drinking water on site.
Heads up — day 2 length depends on where you camp: the full Eichstätt → Kelheim stretch is about 96 km, so day 2 is roughly ~85 km from Inching, ~75 km from Gungolding, or ~65 km from Kipfenberg (plus ~5.5 km more to reach Saal (Donau) in each case). All flat, dedicated path — but worth factoring in when deciding how far to push on day 1.

Train + bike notes: agilis (operator of RB17/RE18) accepts the Deutschlandticket for the passenger fare, but it does not cover the bike. Each bike needs its own ticket — a "Fahrrad-Tageskarte Bayern" or "Fahrradkarte Nahverkehr," both about €7.50. There's no reservation system and no guaranteed space: bikes are first-come and get bumped in favour of wheelchairs/strollers if the train is full, so a summer Thursday afternoon carries some risk of a full train — worth having a backup (next hourly departure, or the direct-cycle fallback to Regensburg described below) in mind.

Saal (Donau) departures toward Ingolstadt run roughly hourly through the evening: 14:08, 15:14, 16:08, 17:14, 18:08, 19:14, 20:08, 21:14 (weekdays) — plenty of buffer even if day 2 runs long. Ingolstadt → Nürnberg regional trains are frequent (roughly every 30–60 min).

Alternative: the boat route (kept for reference)

An earlier version of this plan had day 2 ending with the Kelheim → Weltenburg boat through the Donaudurchbruch gorge, then a cycle on to Regensburg for the train home. That's a nicer scenic finish (and a good excuse to visit Kloster Weltenburg's monastery brewery) but adds boat-schedule and water-level dependency. The Saal (Donau) route above is simpler and fully train-based — worth keeping the boat option in mind only if day 2 is going well and there's still energy/time for the detour.

  • Kelheim–Weltenburg boat schedule (Donaudurchbruch) — daily in season, bikes €5.50, water-level dependent
  • Google Maps: direct Kelheim → Regensburg cycling fallback (~38 km)

Tickets & logistics

  • Bike + train (Bayern regional lines): ~€1/bike on DB regional trains with a Bayern-weit ticket (e.g. Bayern-Ticket). On agilis (RB17/RE18) it's a separate ~€7.50 bike ticket instead — see above.
  • Rush-hour rule: no bikes on S-Bahn/regional trains Mon–Fri 6:00–8:00. An 8:00 departure clears this.
  • Camping: no booking needed for the Bootsrastplätze (first-come); AZUR Camping Kipfenberg is a proper campground and worth a quick call ahead in July's high season.
