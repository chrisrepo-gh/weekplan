Altmühltal Bike Tour

Erlangen → Treuchtlingen → Kipfenberg → Kelheim → Regensburg → Erlangen · 2 days · normal fitness, first bike tour

Plan at a glance: train out to Treuchtlingen, cycle the flat, dedicated Altmühltal-Radweg for two days with an overnight in Kipfenberg, finish the valley in Kelheim, cross the Danube gorge by boat to Kloster Weltenburg, cycle on to Regensburg, and train home. Point-to-point the whole way — no backtracking.

Day 1 — Wednesday

TimeLeg
08:00Erlangen → Nürnberg Hbf (S1, ~12 min)
~08:30–09:00Change at Nürnberg Hbf → Regional-Express to Treuchtlingen (~55–70 min)
~09:30–10:00Arrive Treuchtlingen, unload bike, start riding
~10:00 onwardCycle Treuchtlingen → Pappenheim → Solnhofen → Dollnstein → Eichstätt → Kipfenberg ~66 km

Flat, car-free, well-signed dedicated path the whole way. Worth a stop in Solnhofen (fossil/Jura-Museum) and in Eichstätt's baroque old town / Willibaldsburg castle en route.

Overnight: Kipfenberg
Small Altmühltal town, roughly the halfway point of the whole valley crossing. Lodging not yet confirmed — options seen so far: Landgasthof zum Raben, Gasthof/Pension "Zum Blauen Hecht", or the local campsite. No confirmed Bett+Bike/hostel-style option found yet — worth a call or a booking-site check before the trip. (For comparison, Eichstätt has an excellent, confirmed Bett+Bike DJH hostel from ~€33/night, but staying there would make day 2 a ~96 km push instead of the balanced ~65 km below.)

Day 2 — Thursday

TimeLeg
~09:00Depart Kipfenberg
Cycle Kipfenberg → Beilngries → Dietfurt → Riedenburg → Essing → Kelheim ~65 km
~13:00–15:00Arrive Kelheim, board the Kelheim–Weltenburg boat bikes allowed, ~€5.50/bike, daily, ~every 40–50 min
Boat through the Donaudurchbruch (Danube gorge) to Kloster Weltenburg ~20 min, oldest monastery brewery in the world
Cycle Weltenburg → Regensburg ~15–20 km, flat Danube path
Train Regensburg Hbf → Nürnberg Hbf (RE, ~1 hr) → Erlangen (S1, ~12 min)

Comfortable buffer before evening even with stops at Riedenburg (dinosaur/crystal museum, Rosenburg falconry castle) and Weltenburg Abbey.

Tickets & logistics

  • Bike + train: ~€1/bike on regional trains with a Bayern-weit ticket (e.g. Bayern-Ticket, which also covers the passenger fares for up to 5 people). No reservation needed, first-come space in the multi-purpose car.
  • Rush-hour rule: no bikes on S-Bahn/regional trains Mon–Fri 6:00–8:00. An 8:00 departure clears this.
  • Boat: Kelheim → Weltenburg runs daily in season (roughly May–Oct), bikes €5.50 one-way. The longer Kelheim/Regensburg direct boats only run on specific weekdays (Tue or Sat depending on operator) — not usable for a Thursday, hence the boat + short cycle combo instead.
  • Book lodging ahead — July is high season in the Altmühltal.

Packing checklist

  • Helmet
  • Repair kit: spare tube, pump, tire levers, multitool
  • Light lock
  • Rain layer
  • Sun protection + water bottles
  • Phone battery pack
  • Cash + card
  • Swimwear (river spots along the Altmühl and Danube)
